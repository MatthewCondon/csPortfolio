I have learned a lot in my Computer Science journey...

After two years of my teacher convincing me to take the class, I elected to enroll in A.P. Computer Science A as a junior. I did not take A.P. Computer Science Principles, so I was a little worried about how easy Computer Science would be for me. I can confidently say the transition was not as difficult as I envisioned and I have learned a lot so far.

What I Know:

The first language I learned was Java while in A.P. Computer Science A. Over the course of the year, I started working more with Javascript and even designed some projects with it. During the Summer before my senior year, I began to self-teach Python and C++. I am still trying to learn them right now. I am also comfortable coding with HTML and CSS, which I began to learn in Advanced Computer Science.

My Goals and Plans:

Last Summer, I attempted to teach myself Python and C++ in preparation for Computer Science and Cyber Operations in my college studies. It was challenging and I have not yet mastered the languages. However, that has not stopped me and I will continue my studies. I would also like to begin learning SQL if I find the time this year. Finally, I want to continue designing websites and creating projects so that I can practice my skills.
