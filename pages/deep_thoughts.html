September 24, 2021 - Poptarts vs Tacos
You probably think this is a weird concept. How could a Poptart be classified as a taco? Just think about it, though. How is a taco formed? You have the shell that goes under and rises on the sides. You also have all the "good stuff" on the inside and salsa on top. Think about how a Poptart is created now. You have the crust that goes under and rises on the sides. You get all the "good stuff" on the inside. Lastly, you have got the frosting to top it all off. Don't believe me? Look up a picture of a Poptart and Taco. You will see where I am coming from.


September 29, 2021 - Immovable Object vs All-powerful God
I find this to be an interesting paradox. Let's assume there is an all-powerful God that can do anything. Anything that he imagines is possible can happen. There is someone who asks that God to create an immovable rock. This is where the paradox comes into play. The God can create that rock, but then the God would not be all-powerful in that they couldn't move the rock. The God can also choose to not create the rock, but then they would not be all-powerful in that they could not make the immovable rock. So the question stands: Can an all-powerful God create an immovable object?


October 19, 2021 - The Law of Conservation of Matter
Let us first remind ourselves what the Law of Conservation of Matter is: matter cannot be created nor destroyed; in a closed system, it will be used again at some point. Given that Earth is a closed system, does that mean I was once a dinosaur? Were you once a winning lottery ticket? If matter will always be reused in our Earth's closed system, and knowing that matter can change forms, theoretically, we were all something else at some point in history.
